Preface§0

This proof is the state of what we know about antibody transport across the blood–brain barrier, bound to the conditions under which each claim holds. It is portable: another reviewer can open it, re-run it, and get the same eight findings with the same 247 evidence atoms. It is correctable: when a new study lands, the condition it weakens is marked, not the headline.

The summary is not the work. The conditions are the work.

Findings§2

01 f-014 Preserved

TfR-targeted antibodies cross the blood–brain barrier via receptor-mediated transcytosis, when receptor density on cerebral endothelium exceeds the monovalent binding threshold.

Holds when TfR density ≥ 3.2 × 10⁴ receptors/cell and affinity is in the monovalent 10–100 nM window and the model is murine or cynomolgus. Breaks when affinity is < 5 nM or geometry is bivalent — see finding 02.

02 f-015 Watch

Monovalent TfR antibodies preserve transcytosis; bivalent designs trigger lysosomal sorting and quench delivery.

Holds when the antibody is engineered for 1:1 stoichiometry at the luminal surface. Weakens when the construct includes avidity-boosting scaffolds (bispecific T-cell engagers) — the receptor-cluster threshold moves and this claim does not yet generalise. Flagged as watch pending replication in the Hultqvist 2024 cohort cit: 22.

03 f-016 Lost on review

Cynomolgus pharmacokinetics predict human ADME for TfR-targeted biologics.

Does not hold. Condition species-conservation of TfR glycosylation was shown false in Ullman 2023 cit: 38 — human TfR shows a sialylation pattern not present in cyno, which shifts the receptor-density threshold by ~2.4×. Claim lost; revisit when humanised rodent data arrives.
