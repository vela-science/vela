Chronic poorly-controlled type-2 diabetes is associated with increased blood–brain barrier permeability to small solutes in APOE ε4 carriers over 65.

Evidence

14 atoms · 11 support · 3 contradict
cohort · DCE-MRI · n = 342 · 2024
supports

APOE ε4 carriers with HbA1c > 8 showed elevated Ktrans in hippocampal capillaries relative to ε3 controls (p = 0.003, n = 342).

Nation et al. · Nat Med 30, 472 · § Results, fig 2b weight 0.22 · quality A
prospective cohort · UK Biobank · 2023
supports

BBB-leak score rose 1.4 SD per decade of diabetes duration among ε4/ε4 subjects, with no equivalent slope in ε3/ε3 (interaction p = 0.008).

Lyu et al. · Brain 146, 3115 · table 3 weight 0.18 · quality A
post-mortem · n = 58 · 2022
contradicts

No significant difference in tight-junction protein expression (claudin-5, occludin) by diabetes status after adjustment for amyloid load (p = 0.41).

Halliday et al. · Acta Neuropathol 144, 112 · fig 2b weight 0.09 · quality B · condition mismatch: no HbA1c stratification
meta-analysis · 17 studies · 2023
supports

Pooled effect of diabetes on BBB integrity markers: Hedges' g = 0.31 (95 % CI 0.18–0.44), with the strongest effects in APOE ε4 cohorts.

Zhang & Iadecola · Lancet Neurology 22, 1045 · fig 3, forest weight 0.14 · quality A
