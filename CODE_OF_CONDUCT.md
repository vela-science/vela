# Code of conduct

Vela follows the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).

## Standards

Behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Treating other contributors with respect.
- Critiquing ideas, not people.
- Accepting that scientific disagreement is the substrate, not a defect.
- Citing prior work and giving credit accurately.
- Reporting and acknowledging mistakes — including in published findings —
  rather than hiding them.

Behavior that is not acceptable:

- Personal attacks, harassment, or discrimination of any kind.
- Falsifying evidence, fabricating provenance, or signing claims you do not
  stand behind.
- Knowingly publishing findings whose evidence cannot be independently audited.
- Using the protocol to attack, defame, or impersonate other researchers.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issue threads, or pull requests.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces — the repository, issue tracker, pull
requests, discussions, and any official Vela communication channel — and
to public spaces when an individual is representing the project.

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behavior to the maintainers at
**will.blair0708@gmail.com**. Reports are reviewed and investigated promptly
and confidentially. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and
security of the reporter.

For security vulnerabilities, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the
offender, deleting comments, banning the offender from the project, or
revoking commit access.

This policy is adapted from the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.1.
